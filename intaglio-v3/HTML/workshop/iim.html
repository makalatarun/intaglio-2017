The Craft of Strategic Leadership

"In the midst of chaos, there is also opportunity." - Sun Tzu

This workshop draws upon the power of cinema to explore the idea of strategy as a process and a journey. It involves an interactive discussion around Akira Kurosawa's 1958 epic movie 'The Hidden Fortress' and what it tells us about the nature of choices made by strategists in the process of conceptualizing and acting upon strategy. This process of making strategic choices on a certain set of dimensions over time is viewed as the craft of strategic leadership.

Key Takeaways
  • What does it mean to conceptualize strategy as a journey involving intertwined choices, rather than as a choice at a given point in time?
  • What are the essential skills to practice the craft of strategic leadership?

Instructors

  • Anirvan Pant is an Associate Professor of Strategic Management at the Indian Institute of Management (IIM) Calcutta. He is primarily interested in studying the processes of identity evolution, legitimation, and category emergence in global strategy. Anirvan’s work has been published in leading scholarly and practitioner journals, including the Harvard Business Review, Journal of International Business Studies, Global Strategy Journal, and the Journal of Product Innovation Management, and has been cited in the Economist. Anirvan was a finalist for the Gunnar Hedlund Award 2010/2011 for the best dissertation in the field of international business. He has received the Outstanding Reviewer Award for 2005–2009 and the FDC Best Paper in Strategy/IB Theory Award 2011 from the International Management Division of the Academy of Management. His courses at IIM Calcutta are usually rated the top by students.
